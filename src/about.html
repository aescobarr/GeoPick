GeoPick

Implementing best practices for georeferencing

GeoPick is an open source online companion tool to the Georeferencing Best Practices (Chapman A.D. and Wieczorek J.R.) that follows its recommendations and practices. Its idea started within work done at the Museu de Ci√®ncies Naturals de Barcelona (MCNB) and at the MOBILISE Cost Action. It is meant to provide georeferencers with a simple, easy-to-use yet powerful tool that help them to follow adequate georeferencing practices, and data standards (i.e. Darwin Core), and its aim is to remain as simple and user-friendly as possible.

FEATURES

Georeferencing method

The tool implements both the point-radius and shape georeferencing methods.

Point-radius method. Once the polygon or line/path have been digitised the tool automatically calculates and displays the centroid and the uncertainty. In the case that the centroid does not fall within the polygon boundaries or on top of the line path, i.e. a concave graphical object, the tool automatically calculates the corrected centre and places it to the nearest point in the georeferenced line or polygon, and the calculates the minimum bounding circle to obtain the coordinates uncertainty.
Shape method. The tool partially implements the shape method. The digitised polygon or line/path are kept and exported in the Darwin Core dwc:footprintWKT format. However, as of this version, the tool does not calculate the the final shape including the uncertainties, yet.

Data export

Data is exported in the Darwin Core format for location data, including the coordinate uncertainty in meters and the digitised shape in the Text format defined by ISO 2016. Data can be easily copied and pasted into a spreadsheet.

On-screen digitisation

The tool allows to digitise polygons or lines, at any zoom level, to respresent descriptions of polygonal and lineal localities. Once done, the vertices of both graphical objects can be edited and moved around.

Layers

Georeferencing can be done using OpenStreet Map, Google streets, Google terrain, Google satellite or Google hybrid.

Site search

The tool uses the Google Maps search API to help do a first approximation to the location of the site to be georeferenced.

Coordinates and scale

The map coordinates are shown when moving the cursor around, and the scale is also shown when zooming in and out. Coordinates are in decimal degrees and scale is shown in either kilometers or meters depending on the zoom level.
